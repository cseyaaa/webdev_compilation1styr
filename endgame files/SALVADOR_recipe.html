PORK SINIGANG (SINIGANG NA BABOY)

What is Sinigang?
        Sinigang is a Filipino meaty soup that is known for being sour and savory, and sometimes spicy. Serve it with rice and it is a complete meal on its own. There are, of course, different ways to prepare it and has a lot of variations. Numerous souring agents can be used aside from the most popular choice wich is tamarind. Guava, santol, kamias, green mango, and calamansi are some of the local fruits that are also used.
        As for the protein: chicken, fish, seafood, and beef are all typically used. But the most common and favored meat for this sour and savory dish is pork.

What cuts of pork to use for Sinigang?
        Any cuts of pork can actually be used for making Pork Sinigang. So it is really up to you what part of the pork you want to use. But the most common cuts are pork belly, pork ribs, and pork neck bones. Pork belly is the most popular choice because of the fat content that makes the broth rich and flavorful. Pork ribs and neck bones are also good choices because of the bones that add flavor to the broth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 pc onion, quartered
  • 2 pcs tomato, quartered
  • 1 pc radish, sliced
  • 1 bundle string beans, cut into 2-inch length   
  • 1 to 2 bundles of kangkong
  • 1 pc eggplant, sliced
  • 10 pcs okra
  • 1 pc green chili
  • 1 pc taro, peeled and sliced
  • 1 pack sinigang mix
  • 1 liter water
  • Â¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • fish sauce, to taste

How to cook Sinigang na Baboy?

  1. Boil water in a pot.
  2. Add onion and tomato. Cook for 5 to 8 minutes.
  3. Put-in the pork. Simmer for 30 minutes or until tender.
  4. Add the taro. Cook for 20 minutes.
  5. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc mix, fish sauce, and ground black pepper; stir.
  6. Put-in the radish and string beans. Cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Add the eggplant, okra, and green chili. Cook for 5 minutes.
  8. Put-in the kangkong. Cover the pot and turn off the heat. Let it stay for 5 minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve with a small bowl with patis and spicy chili.
  10. Share and enjoy!
